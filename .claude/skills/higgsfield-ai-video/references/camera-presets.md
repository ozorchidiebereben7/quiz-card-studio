# Camera Presets Reference

Higgsfield's signature is *named camera motion*. Where most AI video is oddly
static, a deliberately named move makes a clip read as filmed. The rule:
**one move per shot, chosen for the emotional beat.** Stack moves only when a
shot genuinely calls for it (Cinema Studio supports ~3 simultaneous moves) — but
restraint reads more cinematic than chaos.

## How to apply

In the MCSLA prompt, the **C** layer names the move plus its qualities: speed
(slow/rapid), height (eye level / low / overhead), and rig feel (locked /
handheld). Example C layer: `slow dolly-in, eye level, locked-off`.

## The catalog (50+ presets) grouped by intent

### Push / pull (change intimacy or reveal)
- **Dolly In / Super Dolly In** — move toward subject; builds intimacy or tension.
- **Dolly Out / Super Dolly Out / Aerial Pullback** — reveal context; endings.
- **Crash Zoom In / Out** — rapid punch-in/out; shock, comedy beat, hard reveal.
- **Rapid Zoom In / Out**, **YoYo Zoom** — energy, emphasis, rhythmic cuts.
- **Through Object In / Out** — pass through a foreground element as a transition.

### Orbit / arc (emphasize the subject's centrality)
- **360 Orbit** — full circle around subject; hero moment.
- **Arc Left / Arc Right**, **Robo Arm** — sweeping robotic arc; product/hero.
- **Lazy Susan** — subject rotates on an unseen turntable; product showcase.
- **Bullet Time** — frozen subject, camera flies around; action spectacle.

### Lateral / vertical (motion and scale)
- **Dolly Left / Right**, **Pan Left / Right**, **Whip Pan** — lateral moves and
  hard cuts; whip pan hides a transition.
- **Tilt Up / Down**, **Jib Up / Jib Down**, **Crane Up / Down / Over The Head**
  — scale and grandeur; reveal height or environment.
- **Double Dolly** — combined dolly for a layered parallax feel.

### Handheld / energy / grit
- **Handheld**, **Wiggle**, **Buckle Up**, **Road Rush** — realism, urgency,
  documentary or chase energy.
- **FPV Drone** — weave through space at speed; the "impossible" flythrough.
- **Snorricam** — camera rigged to the actor; disorienting, subjective.
- **Low Shutter** — motion-blur trails; dreamy or frantic.

### Lens / perspective effects
- **Dolly Zoom In / Out** (vertigo) — background warps while subject stays;
  unease, realization.
- **Dutch Angle** — tilted horizon; tension, wrongness.
- **Fisheye** — extreme wide distortion; skate/hype energy.
- **Focus Change** — rack focus between planes; direct the eye.

### POV / specialty
- **Object POV**, **Mouth In**, **Eating Zoom**, **Hero Cam**, **Head Tracking**
  — subjective and stylized viewpoints.
- **Overhead** — top-down; god's-eye, patterns, choreography.
- **Static** — locked frame; let performance or a single motion carry it.
- **Hyperlapse**, **Timelapse (Human / Landscape / Glam)** — compressed time.
- **Glam**, **Timelapse Glam** — beauty/fashion polish.
- **Flying Cam Transition**, **BTS** — transitions and behind-the-scenes feel.
- **Incline**, **Car Chasing / Car Grip** — vehicle and slope dynamics.

## Beat → move cheat sheet

| Story beat | Reach for |
|------------|-----------|
| Quiet intimacy / a line lands | Slow Dolly In, Static, Focus Change |
| Sudden reveal / shock | Crash Zoom In, Whip Pan, Dolly Zoom |
| Hero / product money shot | 360 Orbit, Robo Arm, Bullet Time |
| Establishing scale | Crane Up, Aerial Pullback, Tilt Up |
| Chase / high energy | FPV Drone, Handheld, Road Rush, Fisheye |
| Unease / dread | Dutch Angle, Dolly Zoom Out, Snorricam |
| Ending / breathe out | Dolly Out, Aerial Pullback, Overhead |

If unsure, default to a **slow dolly-in** — it's the most reliably cinematic move
and rarely wrong.
